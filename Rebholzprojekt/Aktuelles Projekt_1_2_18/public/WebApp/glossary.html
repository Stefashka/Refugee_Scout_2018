Punctuality in jobs

Punctuality is important in lots of situations in Germany. For example to get to work on time, to pay your bills on time or to meet friends or business partners on time. It can anger people, if you come too late.

Crime

Criminal acts are getting strictly punished and fined in Germany. Even “little” crimes, like not being belted in the car or cycling without light in the night, are getting prosecuted. For thievery, counterfeiting, tax fraud, bodily injury, murder, drug distribution, sexual harassment and violation the consequences go from high monetary penalty to jail sentence. The highest penalty in Germany is life imprisonment, so an imprisonment for undefined time period, but at least 15 years. German politician are discussing about faster deportation of delinquent refugees after the events in Cologne, so it is very important to respect the German law.

Salutation

Meeting people for the first time, it’s usual to shake hands, especially in business meetings or at work. If you meet friends, it’s optional, how you greet them. Germany typically greet friend by hugging them or give them a kiss on the cheek, no matter if boy or girl.

Police

The police in Germany has different functions. If you feel unsafe or discriminated by someone, call the police with the free number 110. Police officers are on the one hand there to protect people in different situations (like...) and on the other hand they want to protect the german law. In case of a crime or criminal action they will arrest you or hand out a punishment (like a penalty and so on). However Police Officers also can be asked if you have a short question about finding a place.
